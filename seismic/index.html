Seismic in Cardiff

Evidence-based design. Reliable delivery.

LEARN MORE

In Cardiff, seismic site assessment addresses the requirements of BS EN 1998-1:2004 (incorporating the UK National Annex) alongside the specific geological conditions encountered across the region. The city’s subsurface is characterised by complex Triassic Mercia Mudstone, Carboniferous Coal Measures, and substantial thicknesses of glacial till and alluvial deposits within the Taff and Ely river corridors. Understanding the dynamic behaviour of these deposits is critical, as the presence of soft, saturated alluvium can amplify ground motions, while historical mining voids and backfilled quarries introduce additional geotechnical hazards. A robust seismic investigation, underpinned by a detailed soil mechanics study, provides the stratigraphic framework necessary to assess liquefaction susceptibility, site classification, and seismic bearing capacity in accordance with Eurocode 8.

Compliance with UK seismic standards demands a rigorous combination of in-situ and laboratory testing to derive the dynamic soil properties required for ground response analysis. We deploy advanced In-Situ techniques, including cone penetration testing (CPT) with pore pressure measurement, which provides near-continuous profiling of tip resistance and sleeve friction essential for identifying liquefiable strata without sample disturbance. This data is directly correlated with results from standard penetration tests (SPT), where calibrated N-values are corrected for overburden pressure and energy ratio to evaluate cyclic resistance ratios. High-quality undisturbed sampling using thin-walled Shelby tubes allows for the recovery of sensitive cohesive soils, preserving their in-situ structure for advanced cyclic testing in our laboratory.

Cardiff’s development landscape presents distinct seismic design challenges, from the high-rise residential and commercial schemes in the city centre to critical infrastructure projects within Cardiff Bay and major transport corridors. The redevelopment of brownfield sites overlying Coal Measures often encounters highly variable ground conditions, where opencast backfill and unconsolidated estuarine silts demand site-specific seismic parameters to avoid overly conservative foundation designs. For waterfront structures, the assessment of strain-softening clays and the potential for cyclic degradation under seismic loading are paramount. These projects rely on the integration of CPT and SPT data to develop ground models that accurately delineate zones of differing seismic response, ensuring foundation systems are engineered for the low-to-moderate seismicity defined for South Wales.

Our seismic investigation process integrates field data acquisition with a targeted laboratory programme, typically including cyclic triaxial or direct shear tests to measure shear modulus degradation and damping ratios under simulated earthquake loading. The final deliverable is a comprehensive Ground Investigation Report providing a full seismic site classification, liquefaction assessment, and derivation of design response spectra. By combining local geological knowledge with strict adherence to UK standards, we deliver a clear, actionable picture of seismic risk, enabling structural engineers to proceed with confidence and avoid the cost implications of unverified assumptions.

Q&A

How much does a CPT in Cardiff cost?

A standard CPT push to 20 m depth in Cardiff typically ranges between £130 and £190 per linear metre, depending on access and the number of dissipation tests. This includes the cone, data acquisition, and a preliminary log within 48 hours. Additional seismic cone or pore pressure sensors may add £15–£25 per metre.

What is the difference between CPT and SPT for Cardiff soils?

CPT provides a continuous profile of cone resistance and sleeve friction, which is ideal for the layered alluvium and glacial till found in Cardiff. SPT gives discrete blow counts at 1 to 1.5 metre intervals, which can miss thin layers. In soft clays, SPT often returns low blow counts (N < 5) that are hard to interpret, whereas CPT measures the full strength profile. We recommend CPT for settlement and liquefaction assessments, and SPT when gravel or cobbles prevent cone penetration.

Do I need a CPT for a residential extension in Cardiff?

It depends on the ground conditions. If your extension is on the alluvial plain near the River Taff or in Cardiff Bay, the soft clay and high water table make a CPT valuable for bearing capacity and slab design. For extensions on the Glacial Till in northern Cardiff, a cheaper option like a hand-dug test pit with a plate load test is often sufficient. We can advise after a site walkover.
